What is Sociology?

The textbook from my introductory sociology class defines sociology as "the scientific and systematic study of groups and group interactions, societies and social interactions, from small and personal groups to very large groups." (Soc3e, section 1.1)

Here are some important vocabulary words to get us on the same page:

  • Society
    • a group of people who live in a defined
      geographical area who interact with one
      another and who share a common culture
  • Culture
    • a group's shared practices, values and beilefs.
    • Culture is so much more than food, holidays and clothes. (thought they are a part of it!)
      It also includes unspoken and unconscious rules.
  • Social Institutions
    • patterns of beliefs and behaviors that meet
      social needs and organize social life
    • Examples: Education, Family, Religion, Government
  • Micro-Level
    • a small unit of analysis
    • Example: Sociologist studying individuals communication
      styles and how it shapes their relationships.
  • Meso-Level
    • a medium unit of analysis
    • Example: Sociologist studying communication patterns
      in groups or organizations and how it influences group dynamics.
  • Macro-Level
    • a large unit of analysis
    • Example: Sociologist studying how language evovles
      over time and how it effects cultural identity.
